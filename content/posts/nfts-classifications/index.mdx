### TL;DR
- NFTs are growing in popularity, and mainstream adaptation
- There will be billions of NFTs representing many things
- Classifying NFTs as first class, business class, and economy class NFTs will deciding which NFTs to own
- The best NFTs long term will be the ones you earn and not the ones you buy
- Medallion class NFTs are the ones you earn and level up your status 

NFTs represent art, game items, music, domain names, books, trading cards, real estate, luxury goods, rewards, and many more. Innovation and technology around NFTs are rapidly advancing to enable more and more use cases. Intrinsic properties such as uniqueness, identity, status, and ownership of NFTs are increasing the utility, price, and value of NFTs for their owners.

In the future, more things are going to be available as NFTs. New business strategies such as “free to own” will enable creators and brands to give away NFTs for free with no transaction cost. Startups are building innovative solutions to reduce the friction in onboarding billions of users to own NFTs. 

There will be many kinds of NFTs for different types of usage. It will be time-consuming and confusing to understand the utility value of NFTs and make decisions to own them or not. A simple classification like we have for travel tickets, such as First Class, Business Class, and Economy Class, will help us classify NFTs to understand their utility value, and make decisions to own them.

Here is how you can classify NFTs. 

![NFT Classifications](./nft_classifications.png)

### First Class NFTs
Price Range: 100,000 to Millions

First Class NFTs are expensive one-off arts, collectible profile pictures (PFP), most popular videos or photos, the first written tweet, or source code.    

Here is a small list of First Class NFTs and prices they sold
- Beeple’s digital art ‘Everydays: The First 5000 Days’ : $69.3 Million
- CryptoPunk #7523: $11.8 Million
- World Wide Web Source Code – $5.4 Million
- First Tweet: $2.9 Million
- Charlie Bit Me YouTube Video: $761,000
- Disaster Girl Meme: $500,000

First Class NFTs mostly show off financial status. They are speculative investments bought to be sold for higher prices in the future. You must not expect any utility in First Class NFTs. 

### Business Class NFTs
Price Range: 1,000 to 100,000

Business class NFTs provide full IP rights to the underlying asset with other benefits like access to an elite community of those NFT holders. Business class NFTs help you to build a business or brand around that NFT. You can think of purchasing Business Class NFTs as an upfront capital investment for your business.

Here is a list of business class NFTs and their utility value.
- Bored Ape Yacht Club (BAYC) NFT: As an owner of BAYC NFT, you have complete IP of the underlying digital art. You can create a business like selling T-shirts and accessories featuring the ape you own.
- Digital Lands and Real Estate NFTs are other example of business class NFTs that allow you to build or rent it out to earn income.

### Economy Class NFTs
Price Range: 10 to 100

Economy class NFTs are more affordable and provide ownership for many things. You own your favorite movie, animation character, community membership, and many others as economy class NFTs. These NFTs are not scares, but you get some utility by owning these NFTs.

Example of economy class NFTs are:
- Warner Bros Lord Of The Rings Movie NFTs
- Superman Comic NFTs 
- Reddit community NFTs
- The Disney Pixar Pals NFT Collection
- Battle Infinity – Popular NFT Rewards Game

The above classes of NFTs are becoming popular and mainstream. You decide to buy and own these First Class, Business Class, or Economy classes NFTs depending on your affordability, interest, beliefs, culture, and other aspects.

A new class of NFTs are emerging in Web3 and Metaverse journey. These NFTs are “Free-to-own” refers to a strategy whereby the creator or brand give away an unlimited supply of NFTs for free.

### Medallion Class NFTs
Price Range: Mostly Free 

These are level up NFTs that are mostly “Free-to-own” or you pay a small gas fee for mint them. Examples of medallion class NFTs are loyalty rewards, digital coupons, video games, memberships access etc. These free or low gas priced NFTs provide entry level access to a brand loyalty program, community membership, game ownership, or an event.

- Brand loyalty rewards will be NFTs. In the case of loyalty rewards, you spend more time and money with a brand. Such as buying more products or participating in brand promotional events to level up your membership from silver to gold to get more discounts.
- Another example of medallion class NFTs is entry-level community membership. Your membership status in the community increases as you contribute. Your NFT will provide additional benefits and power to you within the community to moderator content or to vote. 

Medallion class NFTs provide real utility for their holders. Other classes of NFTs intentionally limit the supply of NFTs to create scarcity. In Medallion class NFTs, status is scarcity.

Medallion class NFTs are a great starting point to own NFTs from different communities, brands, and services. Medallion class NFTs allow you to evaluate the benefits of those NFTs by gradually increasing your spending or efforts to level up with a brand or community. The value of medallion class NFTs will increase as your status grow to provide more benefits.

### Conclusion:
The best NFTs long-term will be the ones you earn and not the ones you buy.
